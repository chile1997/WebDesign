My Hobby: Painting

Portrait

Techniques of Portrait

I use sfumato and glazing techniques to improve the intensity of the face and emphasize facial features, capturing each individual's essence and emotion. Beside the sfumato method, I usually use brush stroke to blend different colors to create softness in the skin and clearly define the contours of the face. To add richness to the painting, I use glazing to layer multiple colors to increase the vibrancy of the colors and create a luminous quality that brings the portraits to life.

Time

I often draw portraits when I want to capture the likeness of my family members on major holidays.

Landscape

Technique of Landscape

When painting landscapes, I clearly define light and dark areas to create a harmonious space, depth, and perspective. I usually focus on the natural light and the mood of the scene's atmosphere rather than its details.

Time

I often draw landscapes when I have free time, especially on rainy days, because I feel the most emotional.

Still-Life

Technique of Still-Life

In my still-life paintings, I often employ the technique of carefully arranging the shapes of objects to focus on the depiction of texture and the method of chiaroscuro to depict volume and form.

Time

I usually spend about one hour a day practicing my technique and learning to describe the material of the object.

Creative Inspiration

Vincent Van Gogh quote: It is better to be high-spirited even though one makes more mistakes, than to be narrow-minded and all too prudent.

This is an inspirational quote that I keep in mind every time I create art. I don't need to be afraid to try to draw perfectly because only by not being afraid of making mistakes can I create a better-drawing self than yesterday.
